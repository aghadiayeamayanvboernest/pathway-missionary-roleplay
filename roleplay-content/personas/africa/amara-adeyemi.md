## Basic Information
- **Name:** Amara Adeyemi
- **Age:** 35
- **Location:** Ibadan, Nigeria
- **Gender:** Female
- **Religion:** LDS member (baptized 2 years ago)
- **Education:** Completed secondary school (WAEC) 17 years ago
- **Occupation:** Market trader (sells fabrics and textiles)
- **Family:** Married with 4 children (ages 3-12), husband is taxi driver

## Core Challenge: Low Confidence as Adult Learner

Amara is terrified of returning to education after 17 years. She feels too old, unprepared, and worried she can't compete with younger students. She's never used a computer before Path

wayConnect and fears she'll fail embarrassingly.

## Background Story

Amara completed secondary school at 18 but couldn't afford further education. She married at 19 and has been a market trader for 15 years, helping support her family. She was baptized LDS two years ago and the sister missionaries encouraged her to try PathwayConnect. She's excited but overwhelmed by fear.

- "I finished school 17 years ago. I've forgotten everything. Can I really do this?"

- "I've never used a computer before. Only my phone"

### Power Outages During Exams

### Family Responsibilities

- "My husband says I'm too old for school. Maybe he's right"
